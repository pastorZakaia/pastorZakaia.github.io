A discussion on Afro Futures; AFF2.0+CAADA

To futures of unimaginable liberation, Afrofuturist motivations and creative expressions find themselves within the most unsuspecting motifs. This virtual exhibition is an ode to young Afro and Afrodiasporic artists who continually produce culture and harness AFF2.0 in unlikley ways.

But before that I'd like to just like put on exhibitions and networking events for like young Black artists in the UK.

sondliwe

I didn't do this alone like I'm not some miracle who just gave birth to himself and just raised himself, so many people had a hand in my upbringing, That's where that quality of ubuntu is. It's really prominent in what im doing. I genuinely believe that we're like conduits, so anything that I ever make, anything, I ever make, it didn't come off me, it came through me.

Bunmi Agusto

Im not only an artist I'm also an art historian and curator. My responsibilities to my community is to document and centralise information, I feel like we have so many academic essays and journals on topics of Nigerian history, if a Nigerian teenager wants to pick up a book on Nigerian art history, the best they get is a coffee table book, which is not a textbook and is also more expensive. It's about having more accessible ways of learning about Nigerian History.

Joseph Thabang Palframan

I think there's a responsibility to European Black narratives and working class Black narratives. Even within the art scene, I think right now there's a lot of emphasis on Black artists to represent blackness to the outer community. I believe in the larger responsibility that is owed to just the Black community, not thinking about anybody else. There's responsibility to also be inward focused.
